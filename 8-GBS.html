How Genotyping-by-sequencing (GBS) Works

GBS is marker-assisted selection (MAS) technology to accelerate plant breeding. This technology con-sists is the sequencing of a reduce representation of an entire genome. The sequencing is performed on samples from a population of interest, and this population is selected based on its variability for a certain set of yield traits or disease responses.

A reduce representation of the genome is obtained through digesting DNA samples with some restriction enzymes, then adaptors are ligated and library size is selected and sequenced. Focusing on those part of the genome around restriction enzyme, simplify data cost and complexity without sacrificing genotyping capabilities.

After samples are sequenced, they go through a comprehensive bioinformatic pipeline that ultimately can associate markers (or mutations) from different samples with phenotypic data, leading to the identi-fication of high-resolution molecular markers related with yield traits or disease responses.

  • Demultiplexing & quality control:
    • Deconvolute sequencing library data into multiple samples
    • Insert length determination
    • Contamination removal
    • Low quality sequence trimming
    • Sequence quality report
  • Map reads to the reference:
    • Alignment of short-reads
    • BAM manipulation
    • Quality and coverage mapping report
  • Markers detection
    • Multi sample variants detection
    • Variants filtering
    • Genotype imputation
    • Functional annotation of genomic variants
    • Summary variants report
